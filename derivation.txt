Derivation — Compound Poisson Process S(t)

We consider the compound Poisson process
S(t) = X1 + X2 + ... + X_{N(t)},
where:
• N(t) ~ Poisson(λ t)
• Xi are iid Exponential(μ)
• N(t) is independent of Xi.

----------------------------------------------------
1. Laplace Transform


The Laplace transform of S(t) is:

L_S(t)(u) = E[ e^(−u S(t)) ]
          = exp( − λ t * (u / (μ + u)) ).

----------------------------------------------------
2. Moment Generating Function (MGF)

Using the relation M_S(t)(v) = L_S(t)(−v):

M_S(t)(v) = exp( λ t * (v / (μ − v)) ),   for v < μ.

----------------------------------------------------
3. Mean and Variance


Mean:
E[S(t)] = (λ t) / μ

Variance:
Var(S(t)) = 2 λ t / μ^2

----------------------------------------------------
4.PDF for s > 0


For s > 0, the density of S(t) is:

f_S(t)(s) = exp( −(λ t + μ s) )
            * (λ t μ) / sqrt(λ t μ s)
            * I1( 2 * sqrt(λ t μ s) ),

where I1 is the modified Bessel function of the first kind.

----------------------------------------------------
5. PDF of S(t) at Zero

The distribution has a point mass at zero:

P( S(t) = 0 ) = exp( − λ t ).

----------------------------------------------------
6. Notes


• S(t) has a mixed distribution: a point mass at 0 and a continuous density for s > 0.
• For large values of λ t, S(t) is approximately Normal with:
      mean = λ t / μ
      variance = 2 λ t / μ^2
• S(t) is a classical model in queueing systems, risk theory, and renewal processes.
